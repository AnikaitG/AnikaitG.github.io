{abnoName} is a young boy with skin that resembles a starry night sky, and a galaxy visible in its hair. It wears a black tank top and grey pants that are torn at the bottom, with a hole in the left pant leg.

The child has big blue eyes that usually carry a tearful look. The Abnormality has a fairly shy demeanor, but displays a great interest in forming bonds with others. When {abnoName} considers a person as its friend, it will gift them a white, glowing pebble as a token of their friendship.

{abnoName} is extremely protective of its friends. It becomes jealous whenever one of their friends spend time with someone else, paranoid that they are being replaced and that the friend will leave them. The pebble allows the child to spy on the friend, keeping track of their movements and actions, and to also whisper to them.

Lobotomy Corporation

Lobotomy Corporation

In containment, {abnoName} deals 2-3 {BLACK} damage and produces a maximum of 16 {E-Boxes}.
It responds to all four work orders equally:
{Instinct} (Common) = {Insight} (Common) = {Attachment} (Common) = {Repression} (Common).

When an employee first finishes a work with {abnoName}, they will receive a pebble from the Abnormality for that day. The pebble will continously heal the employee's health and sanity, the amount scaling in proportion to the Qliphoth Counter.
When an employee who is in possession of a pebble finishes a work with {abnoName}, it will increase the Qliphoth Counter, further boosting the healing effects of the pebble.
If the employee works with a different Abnormality, they will instead receive damage from the pebble during the work. This damage also scales proportionally to {abnoName}'s Qliphoth Counter. Once the work is finished, the child's Qliphoth Counter will decrease.

Its Qliphoth Counter starts at 1, but can be increased to a maximum of 5. Upon reaching 0, all employees holding a pebble will instantly die and {abnoName} will fall to is knees and begin crying loudly.

Managerial Guidelines
  • When an employee finished the work, they received a Token of Friendship. This constantly healed the employee's HP and SP relative to {abnoName}'s Qliphoth Counter.
  • When an employee with a Token of Friendship finished the work, the Qliphoth Counter increased.
  • When an employee with a Token of Friendship worked with another Abnormality, they received damage relative to {abnoName}'s Qliphoth Counter, which then decreased.
  • When an employee with a Token of Friendship died, the Qliphoth Counter decreased by 4.
  • When the Qliphoth Counter reached 0, all employees with a Token of Friendship died.

A boy with big eyes, and skin that resembles the starry night sky. It is fairly shy, but doesn't show any fear or hostility towards others. It becomes very friendly after forming a certain level of friendship with someone. Some employees have a purely favourable impression of this Abnormality.

It largely favours conversing and playing with others, showing a strong attachment to the act of making contact with others.

If the boy reaches a certain level of intimacy with an employee, it will gift a small pebble to its friend. While holding the pebble, the employee feels happiness and vigor. However, the effects will be different if the employee enters any containment unit other than its.

If an employee with the pebble, a token of the child's friendship, dies, the Abnormality shows deep grief for their death.

    {abnoName} appears as the second Abnormality on the {Floor of Art}, unlocking {Breakdown Teardrop}, {Awakening Token of Friendship}, and {Breakdown Pebble} after its suppression.

    In its book, {abnoName} is joined by two Friends to the Galaxy. The child does not directly participate in combat, with the Friends fighting in its stead.
    The Friends cannot be killed, and can only be brought down to 1 HP. In order to complete the suppresion, both of the Friends need to be brought down to 1 HP together. If only one of them is brought to this point, it will become Staggered. If the second friend is not dealt lethal damage within two turns of this, the Friend at 1 HP will recover HP equal to its ally.

    At the start of each turn, both Friends will heal 10% of their HP making it more difficult to bring them both down to 1 HP. Though while a Friend is at 1 HP, it will no longer heal HP for two turns allowing the other Friend to be focused down.
    While a Friend is in this state, {abnoName} will become distressed, falling to is knees and crying. This signifies the activation of the 'Parting Tears' passive which makes all characters (including the Friend) lose 10% of their Max HP.
