Abnormality Story

This place is always like this. So it was just like any other day when an incident led to mass casualities within the facility. The reason was the same as usual. Something escaped, and trying to recontain it led to another containment breach and…
Thankfully, the department was sealed off before things could get any worse, which meant all the deaths happened in the same place. It created a literal mountain of bodies. This was something no one wanted to clean up.
Normally, it would have been taken care of within the day, but due to the horrific nature of the incident, the clean up was delayed with authorisation from the department's Sephirah. Don't ask me which department it was, I don't remember all the details.

When left out in the open, a body will begin to rot within a few hours. Most of the bodies were maimed in one way or another, so they rotted even faster.
I guess you don't know this yet, but most of the employees here are very good at cleaning up dead bodies. Cleaning up over and over again will make anyone an expert, even if they don't want to be. Let's say someone you were chatting with about dinner ends up dying a horrific death. Rather than running away and vomiting, all those repeated experiences will help you clean up instead.

Well, the mountain should have been removed quickly, but it remained in the department for a long time. Nature has its way of rotting, melting and corroding everything.

That's how it began. From the mountain of bodies. Amazing, huh? I've still got more to tell you. The dead fused with one another, and they were all smiling. They shared body parts, at least those that were still intact, and became each other's legs and mouths. But they didn't regain their conciousness. They only had one thing on their mind; flesh. When there's an announcement of someone's death, you must check if this thing's quarantined. Once it gets a whiff of the scent of death, it will move quickly towards it. The smiling faces are eerie and yet full of sorrow.
The more bodies it has, the more defined its shape becomes. And it is never satisfied.

I'm telling you all of this because it's almost here. Accidents always happen in this place. Some may spare a moment of silence to mourn a loss, but most would only say “Again?” and turn their backs. There is one thing we did right though, and that was quarantining the department. You see, I'm the only one left. Did you learn anything from this story? What kind of lesson can be learned from a story as horrible as this, you ask? It's similar to most fairy tales. The lesson is don't be lazy. Once my voice stops, after exactly three minutes, come here and clean up your colleagues.

I probably will have to stop speaking soon. I'm about to swallow a large bomb. Time's running out. Blood is flowing all around here. I see all the smiling faces here… It's something you don't get to see much of around here.
Don't be surprised when you hear a loud explosion. Remember, precisely three minutes. I'm sure a bomb this size will be able to suppress it.
The last body to fall will be mine.
