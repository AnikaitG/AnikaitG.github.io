{abnoName} is a wooden marionette with a thin body and lanky limbs. It has hollowed eyes and a mouth carved out at the bottom of its head which also acts as a slot to connect the head to the neck. Three long, thin strings are attached to the top of its head, and loosely fall behind it like hair. A marionette controller hangs above it, with chains linking it to the Abnormality.

{abnoName} has a tendency to observe others and learn from them, and by doing so also learns to copy them. It mimicks people in the hopes that it can be a person too. However, due to this, it also learned to lie. By lying, its long nose grows even longer and impales people.

Library of Ruina

{abnoName} in Library of Ruina

{abnoName} appears as the third Abnormality on the {Floor of General Works}, unlocking {Awakening Learning}, {Breakdown Lies}, and {Breakdown Curiosity} after its suppression.

In its book, {abnoName}'s hands are replaced by axes. It is also joined by two smaller copies of itself. All three {abnoName}s lack proper decks and instead they each copy the deck of a different opponent. While {abnoName} the Curious is alive, the cost of every page will be randomized. And while {abnoName} the Copycat is alive, all {abnoName}s gain either {LoR SE b Strength} or {LoR SE b Endurance} each turn.

Every 3 turns, {abnoName} will tell a Lie, by using a page from the copied deck that displays false information. On this turn, a random librarian will receive a copy of 'Lying is Bad!' which must be used to clash against the page containing the lie.
If the lie is correctly identified and busted, {abnoName} will lose 4 Power ensuring a won clash. All {abnoName}s will receive stat debuffs the following turn and have their Stagger resistances set to "Weak".
However, if the lie is not busted, {abnoName} will instead gain 4 Power ensuring a lost clash. Additionally, all {abnoName}s will receive heavy stat buffs the following turn.
